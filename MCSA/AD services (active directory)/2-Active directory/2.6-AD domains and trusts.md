**Trust** --> like a "bridge" between two different domains. It allows users from one domain to access resources (like files or printers) in another domain.![[../../../attachments/Pasted image 20260303204252.png|Pasted image 20260303204252.png]]
# forest trust vs external trust 
- forest trust --> A bridge between two **entire forests**. It is "transitive," meaning if Forest A trusts Forest B, all sub-domains in A trust all sub-domains in B.
first before we make trust ,, must make DNS and conditional forwarder .  EX :"Connecting all of `cita.local` to all of `cisco.com`."

- external trust --> A bridge between two **specific domains** only. It is "non-transitive." It doesn't connect the whole forest, just those two specific points.  ex : "If you only want `cita.local` to talk to `cisco.com`, but NOT their sub-domains".

# what is direction of trust two way & one-way incoming & one-way outcoming  ?
- two-way-trust --> Users in `cita.local` can use resources in `cisco.com`, AND users in `cisco.com` can use resources in `cita.local`. 
-  **One-Way: Incoming** -->Users in **your** domain (`cita.local`) can go over to the other domain and use their files. But their users cannot come to yours.
- **One-Way: Outgoing**-->Users from the **other** domain can come into `cita.local` and use your resources. Your users cannot go to theirs.
